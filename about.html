The short version

I'm a product designer who spent the first half of my career writing code. That doesn't make me a better designer — it makes me a different one. I obsess over the seams: the moment a click becomes an animation becomes a network call becomes a state. Most design problems hide there.

Today I lead design on AI tools at Lumen. By night I build Notebook.fm, an audio journal for builders, plus eight other indie apps that exist mostly because I can't stop shipping.

"Design is how I think. Building is how I prove it."

How I work

I start with the messy artifact — a screenshot, a Loom, a transcript, a SQL query — never with a Figma file. I ship rough things fast and let real usage tell me where the edges are. The polish comes after the bet has paid off.

I write a lot of words. Most projects start as a memo. If the memo isn't sharp, the design won't be either.
